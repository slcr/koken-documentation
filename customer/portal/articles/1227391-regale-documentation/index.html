Regale

Last Updated: Feb 10, 2014 10:19AM EST

Regale is a bold, assured theme that fluidly scales content across the entire browser window for larger than life photos. Includes a basic header and footer navigation, social media links, plus a configurable grid layout that allows you to control spacing and column layout. Single photos presented on the content page are scaled as large as they can go for maximum impact.

Site title logo replacement

Look for the "Site title" section of the theme settings. Here you can switch the site title in the header from "Text" to "Image" to display a logo. After selecting "Image", paste into the provided field an absolute path to the logo you'd like to use. You can also dictate how large the logo should be when viewed on mobile devices by adjusting its scale.

Header space is tight in this theme, so we recommend displaying predominantly horizontal logo images. If you need more vertical space in the header, open Regale's settings (gear icon at top right of Site) and edit the "Header height" slider in the "Spacing" area. While you're in there you could also adjust the padding and spacing of other options as well.

Style

Regale offers only a single style (white with black text) to keep things simple and minimal. You can however customize the background color of the body, header and footer in addition to general text and link text by scrolling to the bottom the settings panel. If you need additional control over color we recommend using the CSS editor to apply your own style markup.

Spacing

A variety of sliders are offered at the top of "Theme" in the settings panel to control the theme's global header and footer height, inside padding, inside spacing and inside vertical offset. The latter setting is most helpful if you want to change the background color of the body and need to offset content from the header.

Grid layout

Regale's grid is fluid, which means it completely fills every browser window, no matter the size. By default the grid is set to 3 columns for desktops, laptops and tablets, but drops to a single column when viewed on a phone. You may increase or decrease the number of columns in the grid by opening Regale's settings and clicking on the "Template" tab at the top. There you'll see sliders to control column count and spacing (gutter between each image).

Note that the Content, Album, Favorites and Archive Content pages all use the same grid settings (what you see on the front page). The Album and Set pages use their own unique grid settings so they may be styled differently. Once you edit the grid layout for one of these two page types the settings will carry over to their sibling pages as well.

Albums page

The "Albums" page uses the grid layout to display the primary cover of each public album from the Library. Hover over a cover and you'll see the album's title and summary. You can change the background color and opacity of this overlay as well as the color of the text by clicking on the "Template" tab in Regale's settings panel. Also in that area you'll see a "Show titles" select. This lets you choose to remove the overlay or display the title/summary underneath each album.

FYI - because hover states don't exist on mobile, Regale will automatically display album titles and summaries underneath covers when viewed on a tablet or phone.

Also in the Template settings will be an additional setting to control the cover image crop size. It's 3:2 by default, but you are free to change this if you'd like in the "Grid" section at the top.

Splash page

Regale includes a custom splash.lens template that displays a full-screen slideshow of featured content. The template is designed to be used as a splash page for those who'd like to feature content as an introduction to their site.

To use, click "Add Links" in the left column of Site, then click the "Add" button next to "Full screen splash page". Name the page whatever you like, then add it to your navigation. You may then assign the page as the front page of your site (and optionally hide it from the navigation) by following these instructions.

The splash page displays Featured content from the Library by default. Drag-and-drop Library images onto "Featured content" at the top of the left column to choose your images. To sort, click on "Featured content" in the Library then drag-and-drop images into your preferred order.

Instead of Featured content you may also display Favorites or Featured albums from the Library. To use those instead of Featured content, click on the theme settings button (looks like a cog wheel) at the top-right of Site, then click on "Template" at the top of the panel. There you'll see a drop-down to change the slideshow's source.

When using Featured albums, the primary album cover of each album is displayed. You may change the image used by editing album covers in the Library.

The splash page's slideshow is configured to move through images when clicked. If loading Featured albums, you might consider changing this so that the featured album's page loads when its cover is clicked. Or you could set the click to do nothing.. Either way, to edit the click behavior you'd mouseover the slideshow in Site, then click the round slideshow edit button at top right. The slideshow settings panel will open and display the click action setting as well as other configurable options.
